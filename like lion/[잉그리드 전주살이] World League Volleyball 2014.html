[잉그리드 전주살이] World League Volleyball 2014
2014년 07월 03일 (목) 전주대 교수 APSUN@sjbnews.com
June weekends are always too busy for me. This time I had meetings, a volleyball tournament and was very sick the first weekend. June seems to always be the month that the World Volleyball League games come to Korea. I see as many games as I can, in person or on TV. This year I managed to watch the second game against the Czech Republic on TV. It was great. Korea won three games straight. Last weekend I went to Daejeon for the weekend for two days of volleyball. In the World League there are 4 teams in each division and they play two games in their own country and two games in their opponents’ countries. So each pair of teams play for two days in each country. So the games are played Saturday and Sunday and like last year, I got to watch Korea host the Netherlands. Although I am Canadian, I have dual citizenship, the second being Dutch which means from the Netherlands. So I have my Dutch colors, an orange Nike jacket with “Netherlands” on the back and the crowned lion symbol on the front, a blue soccer shirt and an orange bracelet. I brought the clothes and wore the bracelet at the game. It is hard for me to decide who to cheer for so I cheered for both teams on good plays. And there were lots.

This year’s slogan is “This Is Volleyball” and it certainly is. The level of play is incredible. This is not the level where it is simply “serve, receive, set, spike, point”. Of course there are a few points like that. But there were many heart pounding moments where the ball went over and over the net, fantastic dives and running over furniture to get the ball and keep it in play. I wrote on my Facebook page that I needed to remember to breath. I don’t use Facebook much but I did this time. There were new players on each team. The most impressive was Jeon KwangIn. He flew over the net and had lots of power in his serve and spike. Players on both sides fell over from the other team’s power. With the exception of missed serves, each point was hard fought for. Both days were not disappointing. Both sets went to five games. I really thought that Korea would win but the Netherlands prevailed. Some people were upset that Korea lost. I think that if a team plays spectacularly well but loses there is nothing to be ashamed of. Korea’s volleyball players proved themselves to be world class athletes and I am proud of them.

I had invited my Hyperion members ? the volleyball club I belong to ? to join me but I heard no one was coming. So I was surprised to get a call from the vice president that he and another member could see me. I was given a seat in the reserved section by my friends at the Korea Volleyball Association. They gave them day passes so the boys could sit next to me. Jaeoong knows so much about Korean volleyball. He identified a number of VIPs around us. Then the KVA elevated me to a VIP. I was thrilled. I talked to the Dutch statistician about some of the players and the team as he walked past my seat. After the game we went downstairs and the players from both teams walked past us. The guys got some pictures and then Jaeun asked if he could get his picture in centre court in front of the net. Since almost everyone had gone, it was possible and the three of us are in the shot. On Sunday I didn’t notice that my girls’ team left spiker had called ? she was there. It was too late to trade seats but she and her boyfriend came to me after the game. I took them out the same way and they got their pictures with some of the players and they took one of me with two Dutch players. I talked to one of them after for a bit. I told the Korean team coach that his team played incredibly well. My kids thanked me for the opportunity to get near the players and all the pictures. But I would not have had that chance to share if the KVA people had not become friendly with me because we share a love of volleyball that goes beyond borders. I am very grateful to them for a great weekend and the Korean team autographed gold souvenir volleyball. /전주대 교수
